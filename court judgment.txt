JUDGMENT

Date of Judgment: 20th March 2024

1. INTRODUCTION

The present suit has been filed by the Plaintiff seeking recovery of a sum of Rs. 3,00,000 along with interest from the Defendant. The Plaintiff alleges that the Defendant borrowed the said amount as a friendly loan and has failed to repay the same despite repeated requests and issuance of a legal notice.

2. FACTS OF THE CASE

The Plaintiff submits that on 10th January 2023, the Defendant requested a friendly loan of Rs. 3,00,000 for business purposes. The Plaintiff agreed to lend the said amount considering the long-standing relationship between the parties.

The Plaintiff transferred the said amount through bank transfer to the Defendant’s account. The Defendant promised to repay the loan within six months but failed to do so.

The Plaintiff further states that a legal notice dated 1st November 2023 was sent to the Defendant demanding repayment of the loan amount. However, the Defendant neither replied to the notice nor repaid the amount.

3. ISSUES FOR DETERMINATION

The following issues arise for consideration before this Court:

i) Whether the Plaintiff has successfully proved that the Defendant borrowed a sum of Rs. 3,00,000?

ii) Whether the Defendant has failed to repay the loan amount as alleged?

iii) Whether the Plaintiff is entitled to recover the said amount along with interest?

4. RELEVANT LEGAL PROVISIONS

The Court has considered the following provisions of law:

Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 – Compensation for loss or damage caused by breach of contract.

Section 74 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 – Compensation for breach of contract where penalty is stipulated.

Section 34 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 – Interest.

Section 101 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 – Burden of proof.

5. ANALYSIS AND FINDINGS

Upon examining the documentary evidence produced by the Plaintiff, including the bank transfer records and the copy of the legal notice issued to the Defendant, this Court is satisfied that the Plaintiff had indeed transferred a sum of Rs. 3,00,000 to the Defendant.

The Defendant has failed to produce any evidence to rebut the Plaintiff’s claim or to establish that the amount had been repaid.

Under Section 101 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872, the burden of proof initially lies upon the Plaintiff to establish the claim. In the present case, the Plaintiff has successfully discharged this burden by producing credible documentary evidence.

Further, the Defendant's failure to repay the loan amount constitutes a breach of the agreement between the parties. In such circumstances, the Plaintiff is entitled to compensation under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

With respect to the claim for interest, this Court finds it appropriate to award reasonable interest under Section 34 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908.

6. FINAL ORDER

In view of the facts and circumstances of the case, the Court hereby passes the following order:

a) The Defendant is directed to pay the Plaintiff a sum of Rs. 3,00,000.

b) The Defendant shall also pay interest at the rate of 10% per annum from the date of filing of the suit until the date of realization.

c) The Defendant shall bear the costs of the suit.

The suit is accordingly decreed in favour of the Plaintiff.
